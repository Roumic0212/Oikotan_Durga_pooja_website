Durga Puja Bhog

During the grand festivities of Durga Puja, one of the most cherished traditions is the offering of bhog (sacred food) to the goddess. It is a moment when devotion blends with culinary delight, creating a unique experience that encapsulates the essence of this auspicious occasion.

Our Bhog Tradition:

At Oikotan Hebbal Durgotsav, the preparation and offering of bhog hold a special place in our hearts. This tradition dates back generations, and it symbolizes our unwavering commitment to preserving our cultural heritage. It's not just a meal; it's a labor of love, a symbol of devotion, and a testament to our unity as a community.

The Culinary Delights:

Our Durga Puja bhog is a feast for the senses, featuring a delectable array of dishes that range from traditional to contemporary. From fragrant rice to aromatic curries, our bhog is a culinary journey that celebrates the richness of our culture.

Bhog Menu:

Prepare to tantalize your taste buds with a menu that includes:

Khichuri: A flavorful blend of rice and lentils, cooked to perfection.
Chorchori: Fresh and vibrant, showcasing the colors of our traditions.
Roshogolla: Soft, spongy, and soaked in sugar syrup, a Bengali dessert favorite.
Payesh: A creamy rice pudding, enriched with fragrant cardamom and adorned with nuts.
Chatni: Tangy and sweet, this chutney adds a burst of flavor to your bhog experience.
Papod: Crispy, savory snacks that complement the richness of our bhog.
Beguni: Thin slices of brinjal (eggplant) dipped in gram flour batter and deep-fried to golden perfection, offering a delightful crunch.

Preparation and Devotion:

Our bhog is not just about ingredients; it's about the dedication of our volunteers who meticulously prepare each dish with love and devotion. The process is a spiritual journey, and the end result is a divine offering to Goddess Durga.

Join Us for Bhog:

We invite all members of our community and visitors to join us in partaking in this sacred tradition. Share in the blessings of Durga Puja by enjoying the bhog with us. You can also volunteer to be part of the preparation process, experiencing the joy of service and devotion firsthand.
